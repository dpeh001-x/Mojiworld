================================================================
  CLASS PROS & CONS - Mojiworld v0.25.519
================================================================

A field guide to picking your hero. Numbers below are the LIVE
class baselines from the CLASSES table + the v0.25.511 identity
multipliers, so they reflect what you actually see in-game.

Format per class:
  - Baseline stats
  - Class identity (innate perk that always applies)
  - Advancement paths (Job at Lv 20, Master at Lv 40)
  - PROS / CONS
  - Best for
  - Recommended build path


================================================================
  WARRIOR  (the heavy)
================================================================

BASELINE STATS (Lv 1)
  HP:   150  (x1.25 mul = ~187 effective)
  MP:    40
  ATK:   15
  DEF:   11  (x1.20 mul = ~13 effective)
  Crit:  5%
  Speed: 3.2  (x0.90 mul = ~2.88 effective)
  Jump:  10

CLASS IDENTITY
  +25% HP and +20% DEF over baseline. Slowest movement of all
  classes (0.90x). The tank: hardest to kill, hardest to outrun.

JOB PATHS (Lv 20)
  Berserker  - rage mode, lifesteal, +ATK while bleeding
  Knight     - shields, party-defense, Holy Shield invuln+reflect

MASTER PATHS (Lv 40)
  Berserker  -> Warlord     (banner-buff, fan-cleave)
                Doombringer (10x cleaves + slam, pure damage)
  Knight     -> Crusader    (heal + 5 holy orbs + half-damage)
                Dragoon     (leap + slam, mobile bruiser)

PROS
  + Highest HP pool by far (300+ effective at high level)
  + Highest DEF; weathers boss-arena mistakes the best
  + Reliable single-target damage with auto-crit Slash
  + Rich defensive toolkit at every tier (Guardian, Holy
    Shield, Crusader Aegis) - never feels squishy
  + Bloodlust rage = infinite-MP mode (basics produce shockwaves)
  + Most generous "second chance" in fights vs bosses

CONS
  - Slowest of all classes (10% below baseline)
  - Lowest crit ceiling (5% base, no innate %)
  - Mid-range raw ATK (15)
  - Limited AoE in basic kit (Ground Slam is the only big one)
  - Fewest gap-closers - hard to chase a fleeing target
  - Master ults are visually flashy but 16-26 s cooldowns

BEST FOR
  - Players who like to stand and trade
  - Bosses you keep dying to as squishier classes
  - Anyone who values "didn't die" over "topped DPS chart"

RECOMMENDED BUILD
  Beginner:    Berserker -> Doombringer  (highest single-target)
  Tank focus:  Knight -> Crusader        (heal + invuln support)
  Skill toy:   Knight -> Dragoon         (leap-slam mobility)


================================================================
  ROGUE  (the striker)
================================================================

BASELINE STATS (Lv 1)
  HP:    75
  MP:    50
  ATK:   15
  DEF:   3
  Crit:  25%  (x1.20 mul = 30% effective)
  Speed: 5.2  (x1.20 mul = ~6.24 effective)
  Jump:  12

CLASS IDENTITY
  +20% crit chance over baseline. Fastest movement of all
  classes (1.20x). The glass cannon: hits hardest per second
  but folds the moment something connects.

JOB PATHS (Lv 20)
  Ninja      - teleport-strike, sakura petal AoE (Death Blossom)
  Assassin   - smoke / blink-charges (Voidrift Blink x3)

MASTER PATHS (Lv 40)
  Ninja      -> Shadowlord  (3 clones for 15 s, AoE pulses)
                Shinobi     (zip-streak, kanji seal slashes)
  Assassin   -> Nightreaper (eclipse: 10 batches on every visible mob)
                Phantom     (mark 6, chain-execute, ignores DEF)

PROS
  + Highest baseline crit by huge margin (30% effective at Lv 1)
  + Fastest movement / mobility
  + Best gap-closers and disengage tools (Smoke Dash,
    Voidrift Blink x3 charges, Flurry blink, Shadow Strike teleport)
  + Highest single-target burst (auto-crit Shadow Strike, 3-stab Backstab)
  + Eclipse Massacre + Voidrift Execution are both 12 s CD
    (CDR-immune) but huge per cast - legitimate ult spammer
  + Phantom's "ignores DEF + always crits" is the cleanest
    boss-burst kit in the game

CONS
  - Lowest DEF (3 base)
  - Second-lowest HP (75 base, no class HP buff)
  - Fewest AoE tools - struggles vs swarms before unlocking
    Shadowlord clones / Eclipse Massacre
  - Mana-thin at low levels (50 MP base, no innate buff)
  - Voidrift Blink charges encourage over-extending into death
  - Most punishing class for accidental hits

BEST FOR
  - Players who chase top DPS numbers
  - Boss-rush / single-target heavy content
  - Anyone willing to dodge instead of tank

RECOMMENDED BUILD
  Beginner:    Ninja -> Shadowlord    (clones tank for you)
  Boss-burst:  Assassin -> Phantom    (highest per-cast damage)
  Mob-clear:   Assassin -> Nightreaper (every-mob-on-screen ult)


================================================================
  MAGE  (the caster)
================================================================

BASELINE STATS (Lv 1)
  HP:    70
  MP:   130  (x1.50 mul = 195 effective)
  ATK:   10
  DEF:   2
  Crit:  8%
  Speed: 3.4  (baseline, x1.0)
  Jump:  10

CLASS IDENTITY
  +50% MP over baseline. Lowest HP / DEF / ATK of any class
  but compensates with the deepest mana pool and the most
  tools per kit. The sustain caster.

JOB PATHS (Lv 20)
  Archmage   - elemental AoE, chain-cast multipliers
  Warlock    - HP-drain, curses, life-leech
  Priest     - holy, healing, AoE chip + cleanse  (v0.25.512+)

MASTER PATHS (Lv 40)
  Archmage   -> Sage         (5-meteor shower, 19 s CD)
                Elementalist (4-element prismatic cascade, 5 s CD spam)
  Warlock    -> Lich         (30 s vortex pool, sustained drain)
                Hexmaster    (freeze every enemy 2 s + DoT)
  Priest     -> Arch Bishop  (untargetable + 7 holy pillars + MP refund)

PROS
  + Largest MP pool by far (195+ at Lv 1)
  + Best AoE coverage in the game (Arcane Burst, Fireball
    explode, Dark Pulse, Cascade, every master ult)
  + Dimensional Warp (C key) = teleport + AoE damage in one,
    1.3 s CD - cleanest disengage in the game
  + Priest path = self-healing tank-mage (unkillable at high ATK)
  + Elementalist Cascade has 5 s CD - functions like a
    supercharged secondary basic
  + Hexmaster Grand Hex freezes EVERY enemy on screen for 2 s
  + 3 distinct job paths = most build variety

CONS
  - Lowest HP (70) and DEF (2) - one bad hit can be fatal
  - Lowest ATK (10) - low-multiplier skills hit soft
  - Lowest baseline accuracy (90, vs 130 archer) - misses
    more often without ACC investment or Priest buffs
  - High MP costs offset the +50% pool (Holy Grail = 60 MP)
  - Squishy until you unlock Priest sustain or Lich drain
  - Steep learning curve: kit requires positioning + cast order

BEST FOR
  - Players who like deep kits and combo plays
  - Mob-clear / map-grind farmers
  - Anyone wanting maximum class variety

RECOMMENDED BUILD
  Beginner:    Priest -> Arch Bishop    (heal-while-DPS, hardest to die)
  Mob-clear:   Archmage -> Elementalist (5 s CD prismatic spam)
  Sustain:     Warlock -> Lich          (30 s vortex = full screen lock)


================================================================
  ARCHER  (the marksman)
================================================================

BASELINE STATS (Lv 1)
  HP:   100
  MP:    60
  ATK:   13
  DEF:   6
  Crit:  14%
  Speed: 4.0  (x1.10 mul = ~4.4 effective)
  Jump:  11

CLASS IDENTITY
  +10% accuracy / hit rate vs level-gap monsters. Innate
  Eagle Eye toolkit + steady-aim ramps. The kiter: best at
  staying out of melee while picking targets apart.

JOB PATHS (Lv 20)
  Sniper     - long-range, piercing, charge mechanics
  Ranger     - wolf companion, elemental arrows, nature

MASTER PATHS (Lv 40)
  Sniper     -> Marksman    (one-shot one-kill, 12% instakill chance)
                Ballista    (8 s channel piercing arrow stream)
  Ranger     -> Beastmaster (4 oversized wolves, 100 s, 10000 HP each)
                Skyhunter   (10 homing arrows that never miss)

PROS
  + Highest accuracy by 20+ over warrior (130 base)
  + Pierce arrows = best line-AoE in the game (Charged Shot,
    Multi Shot, Marksman, Ballista)
  + Marksman One-Shot has a 12% instakill chance vs non-bosses
  + Beastmaster pack = 40,000 HP of wolves to absorb damage for you
  + Skyhunter "cannot miss" homing arrows = never wasted DPS
  + Solid mid-tier HP (100) and DEF (6) - more durable than
    rogue or mage
  + 4-stack Ranger pet HP (10,000 ea) = best sustained survival
    in long encounters
  + Wolves and pack stay at player Y - good vertical fights

CONS
  - Mid-pack ATK (13) and crit (14%)
  - No innate AoE before Multi Shot at Lv ~5
  - Sniper path lacks survival tools - expects you to kite well
  - Ranger pet only does 1.4x ATK per attack (vs Marksman's 7x)
  - Eagle Eye is a buff, not damage - depends on follow-up
  - Beastmaster pack at 4 wolves gets crowded on tight maps
  - Wild Bond is a 70 s CD - long downtime if your pet dies

BEST FOR
  - Players who like positioning + range control
  - Mixed PvE - wolves carry mob-clear, you handle bosses
  - Anyone who wants "summoner" feel (Beastmaster's permanent
    pack is closest to a real summoner build)

RECOMMENDED BUILD
  Beginner:    Ranger -> Beastmaster   (4 wolves do most of the work)
  Boss-killer: Sniper -> Marksman      (one-shot crits + instakill)
  No-miss:     Ranger -> Skyhunter     (homing arrows = guaranteed DPS)

================================================================
  PICKING SUMMARY
================================================================

  "I want to never die"             -> Warrior
  "I want to top the damage chart"  -> Rogue
  "I want every tool / max variety" -> Mage
  "I want to play patiently"        -> Archer

  "First time playing"              -> Warrior or Mage (Priest)
  "Coming from a souls-like"        -> Rogue (Phantom)
  "I love pets / summons"           -> Archer (Beastmaster)
  "I love combos"                   -> Mage (Archmage Elementalist)
  "I love stat-checking bosses"     -> Archer (Sniper Marksman)

             Z basic, Bash Bar fills bottom-up)

             other class HUD elements - was raised in v0.25.511)
